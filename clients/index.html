Using an IRC Client

Information And Tips

Basic Setup

Network: irc.desertbus.org

Port: 6667

SSL Port: 6697

Autoidentify To Your Donor Account

We provide three methods of automatically identifying to NickServ.

SASL Authentication

SASL Authentication is a secure way to authenticate, and it's one of the easiest to set up if your client supports it. You'll use the email address linked to your donor account as your username, and your donor account password is your password.

Autoidentify to NickServ

Most clients provide a password box to fill in your NickServ password, which is used to send your password to NickServ automatically when you connect to the network. Unfortunately, just your password is not enough to identify you in our unique system. You may fill in your password in this box in one of two ways:

  • email@address.com:password with a colon between the email address and the password
  • email@address.com password with a space between the email address and the password

Autoidentify With The Server Password

Some people prefer to identify using the server password on connect because it activates immediately rather than having to wait for NickServ to respond (not that NickServ is slow, though). Either of the formats given above that can be placed in the NickServ identify box can be placed in the server password box instead. Just make sure not to do both.
